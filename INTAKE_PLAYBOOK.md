# Intake Playbook

Goal: turn a form submission into either a useful free consultation or a fast polite no.

## First reply

Use this when a new request arrives.

```text
Hi <name>,

Thanks for the request. I read your description.

Before we book the free 15-minute consultation, please send me:

1. What are you trying to achieve?
2. What is currently blocked?
3. What have you already tried?
4. What would count as a useful result after one session?

If it looks like I can help, I will send a few time options.

Rustam
```

## Free 15-minute consultation structure

0-3 min:

- Ask for the actual goal.
- Ask what is blocked right now.

3-7 min:

- Identify whether the blocker is knowledge, workflow, setup, project structure, or decision paralysis.

7-11 min:

- Say directly whether a paid session makes sense.
- If not, give the shortest useful next step.

11-15 min:

- If yes, choose plan:
  - `$100`: one 50-minute session
  - `$130`: one 50-minute session plus small questions during the week

## Qualification rules

Good fit:

- The person wants clarity and practical progress.
- The problem is technical, workflow-related, or learning-direction related.
- The person accepts direct feedback.
- The person can describe at least one concrete desired outcome.

Bad fit:

- They want unlimited support for one session price.
- They want emotional reassurance more than technical clarity.
- They expect you to do a whole project for them during a lesson.
- They are not willing to share context or files/screenshots when needed.

## Paid session output

Every paid session should end with:

- what was clarified
- what was changed or solved
- what remains blocked
- next actions
- whether another session is useful

## Support plan boundary

The `$130` plan includes small questions during the week.

It does not include:

- full project work
- long reviews
- debugging without context
- guaranteed instant replies
- replacing another paid session
